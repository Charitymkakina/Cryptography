# Asymmetric Encryption
Asymmetric Encryption is a mathematical operation that can be performed with one key and verified or undone with another key.
One key is kept secret, `Private key` while the other key is shared freely, `public key`

NOTE
`A Public Key`Performs an asymmetric cryptographic operation, and `A private Key` verifies or undoes an asymmetric cryptographic operation.
In `Asymmetric Encryption`, the keys used to perform and verify or undo the operation can sometimes be switched, but that depends on the type of operation.

# Operations Asymmetric Encryptions
1. Encryption (public key: Performs and Private key: Verifies or Undoes)
2. Signatures (Public key: Verifies/undoes while the Private key performs)
3. Key Exchanges (Both keys perform, but this depends on the key algorithms)

# Algorithms
`RSA` `DSA` `DH`
Not every algorithm can do every operation.
1. `RSA` -Encryption, Signatures, and Key exchange
2. `DSA` deals with signatures
3. `DH`- Deals with key exchanges.
